Joint Library Committee

The bicameral committee overseeing the Library of Congress — the world's largest library and Congress's primary research institution.

LIBRARY OF CONGRESSCOPYRIGHTCRSCULTUREBICAMERALRESEARCH

WHY THIS MATTERS

The Library of Congress is not merely a repository — it houses the Copyright Office that shapes U.S. intellectual property policy, and the CRS provides nonpartisan research to every member of Congress. The committee's work affects how copyright law is administered and how Congress accesses independent policy analysis.

WHAT IT DOES

The Joint Library Committee provides oversight for the Library of Congress, including the Congressional Research Service (CRS), the Copyright Office, and the National Library Service for the Blind. It addresses Library operations, digital access, and cultural programs.
