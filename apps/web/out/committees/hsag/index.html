House Agriculture Committee

The Farm Bill — SNAP, crop insurance, commodity programs, and rural development — all start with this committee.

AGRICULTUREFARM BILLSNAPRURALFOOD POLICYCONSERVATION

WHY THIS MATTERS

SNAP feeds roughly 42 million Americans. Crop insurance and price supports determine whether family farms survive bad years. Rural broadband and community development programs depend on Farm Bill funding. The committee's urban-rural coalition is one of the most delicate in Congress.

WHAT IT DOES

The committee writes the Farm Bill, a massive omnibus legislation renewed roughly every five years that covers crop subsidies, food stamps (SNAP), rural lending programs, conservation, and agricultural research. It also oversees the USDA and commodity futures trading regulation.
