House Natural Resources Committee

Federal lands, tribal sovereignty, offshore drilling, and endangered species — the committee that decides what happens to a third of America's territory.

PUBLIC LANDSENVIRONMENTTRIBALENERGYOCEANSWILDLIFE

WHY THIS MATTERS

With the federal government owning roughly 640 million acres, this committee's decisions affect ranchers, miners, tribes, conservationists, and recreationists across the American West. Offshore drilling policy, mineral rights on federal land, and tribal water rights all flow through here.

WHAT IT DOES

Natural Resources oversees federal management of public lands, national parks, forests, oceans, and minerals. It handles policy for Native American tribes, offshore energy leasing, and the protection (or relaxation) of environmental protections on federal land.
