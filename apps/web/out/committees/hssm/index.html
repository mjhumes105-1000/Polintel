WHY THIS MATTERS

Small businesses are often the first job for new workers and the economic backbone of local communities. SBA loans, pandemic relief programs like PPP, and federal procurement preferences for small firms flow through this committee's work. Regulatory reform legislation affecting small businesses also originates here.

WHAT IT DOES

The Small Business Committee oversees the Small Business Administration (SBA) and writes legislation affecting the 33 million small businesses that employ nearly half of American workers. It covers SBA loan programs (7(a), 504, SBIC), federal contracting set-asides, disaster loan assistance, and regulatory burden reduction for small firms.
