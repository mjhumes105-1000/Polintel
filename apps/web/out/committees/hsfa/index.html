WHY THIS MATTERS

Whether Ukraine gets weapons, which countries face U.S. sanctions, whether USAID programs survive — all require action or approval from this committee. As geopolitics grows more volatile, its role in constraining or enabling executive action abroad becomes increasingly consequential.

WHAT IT DOES

The committee oversees the State Department, USAID, and U.S. foreign policy broadly. It authorizes foreign aid programs, arms transfers, economic sanctions, and international agreements. It also conducts oversight of American embassies and diplomatic personnel.
